🔧 Grammar & Phrasing to Improve

You said I am a financial manager in a tax-sold company. → I am a financial manager at a tax consultancy firm. — 'tax-sold' is incorrect; 'tax consultancy' or 'tax advisory' is the correct term.
You said Oh, it's a textile company. Do you know textile? → Actually, it's a textile company. Are you familiar with textiles? — 'Do you know textile' is grammatically incorrect; use 'familiar with' or 'know about'.

Good use of I am a financial manager → **I work as a financial manager** — 'work as' is slightly more natural in spoken English for describing your job role.
