🔧 Grammar & Phrasing to Improve

You said It's a good evening and me → It's a good evening, and I am... — 'me' is an object pronoun; use 'I' as the subject.
You said it's a final intro manager → I'm a financial manager — 'final intro' is unclear; likely meant 'financial'.
You said follow all kinds of procedures → I follow various procedures — 'all kinds of' is informal; 'various' is more precise.
You said the fast-paced work is getting through the fast-paced work → I thrive in a fast-paced environment — repetition and unclear phrasing; use a clearer expression.

⭐ Strong Points & Better Expressions

Good use of I have been working for six years → **I have been working as a financial manager for six years** — adding 'as a...' specifies the role and sounds more professional.
Good use of Actually, I enjoyed the fast-paced job → **In fact, I thrive in a fast-paced environment** — 'thrive' conveys enthusiasm more strongly than 'enjoyed'.
Good use of before I became a financial manager, I am an analyst → **Before becoming a financial manager, I worked as an analyst** — using 'worked as' and past tense improves grammatical accuracy.
