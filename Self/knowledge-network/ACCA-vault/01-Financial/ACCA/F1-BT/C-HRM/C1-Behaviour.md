## 🧠 Big Five (OCEAN) Personality Model

```mermaid
graph TB
    BF[Big Five<br/>Personality Traits]
    BF --> O[Openness<br/>"Curious vs Cautious"]
    BF --> C[Conscientiousness<br/>"Disciplined vs Casual"]
    BF --> E[Extraversion<br/>"Social vs Solitary"]
    BF --> A[Agreeableness<br/>"Cooperative vs Competitive"]
    BF --> N[Neuroticism<br/>"Anxious vs Calm"]
    
    classDef trait fill:#ed7d31,color:#fff
    class O,C,E,A,N trait
```

| Trait | High Score | Low Score | Work Impact |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **O** Openness | Creative, curious, abstract thinking | Practical, traditional | Innovation roles need high O |
| **C** Conscientiousness | Self-disciplined, organised, reliable | Flexible, spontaneous | ⭐ Strongest correlation with job performance |
| **E** Extraversion | Social, talkative, energetic | Solitary, quiet | Sales/management need high E |
| **A** Agreeableness | Cooperative, trusting, altruistic | Competitive, sceptical | Teamwork needs high A |
| **N** Neuroticism | Anxious, emotional | Calm, stable | High-pressure roles need low N |

⚠️ **Research finding**: Conscientiousness is the only trait positively correlated with job performance across virtually all occupations.

## 👁️ Perception Biases

| Bias | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Selective Perception** | Seeing only what supports existing views | Filtering out contradictory evidence |
| **Halo Effect** | One positive trait colours everything | Assuming a well-dressed candidate is competent |
| **Stereotyping** | Generalising based on group membership | "Gen Z can't handle pressure" |
| **Confirmation Bias** | Seeking evidence that confirms prior judgment | Loading interview questions to confirm CV impression |
| **Similarity Bias** | Preferring people similar to oneself | Favouring same-alma-mater candidates |

---

## 😖 Cognitive Dissonance

> Discomfort when behaviour contradicts beliefs → people adjust beliefs to match behaviour

**Workplace examples**:
- Employee knows the product has flaws but must sell it → dissonance → may "convince themselves it's not that bad"
- In turnover research, high-dissonance employees are more likely to leave

### Groupthink

> Suppressing dissent to maintain group harmony → catastrophic decisions

⚡ **Classic case**: Challenger space shuttle disaster (engineers had concerns but were reluctant to voice strong opposition)

**Prevention**: Appoint a Devil's advocate, leader speaks last, bring in external perspectives

### Social Loafing

> Individuals exert less effort in a group than when working alone

**Countermeasures**: Make individual contributions identifiable, reduce team size, enhance task meaningfulness
